03 Process

Every problem leaves clues

Take a closer look!

I approach ambiguous problems like an investigation: follow the evidence, distrust the obvious answer, and don't close the case until the solution holds up under scrutiny. The same method scales from a quick prototype to a multi-quarter platform bet.

01

Gather the evidence

I start with research — interviewing customers, support, and the engineers closest to the problem — to map the system and find the real issue hiding beneath the request.

02

Question the obvious

The first answer is rarely the right one. I separate signal from noise, frame the real bet, and write the narrative that aligns cross-functional partners on scope and success.

03

Test every theory

A good idea has to survive contact with reality. I prototype quickly and pressure-test against real users and skeptical engineers, iterating until the solution feels inevitable.

04

Close the case

I ship, measure the impact, and fold what I learned back into the system — documenting it so the team moves faster on the problem after this one.

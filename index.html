03 The Casebook

How every case gets cracked

Now, I've always said a thorny design problem is no different from a locked-room mystery. The truth is hiding right there in the details — you've only to know where to look, and have the patience to follow the evidence wherever it leads.

01

Examine the Scene

Like any good investigation, it begins at the scene. I question every witness — customers, support reps, the engineer who's been grumbling for months — and hunt for the telling detail everyone else walked right past.

02

Establish the Motive

The obvious suspect is rarely the guilty party. I separate testimony from fact until the real culprit — the problem beneath the ask — finally confesses, then set it down in plain language so every partner agrees on whodunit.

03

Test the Theory

A theory is only as good as the evidence behind it. I prototype quickly and hold each idea up to the lamp — pressing it against real users and skeptical engineers — until only the truth is left standing.

04

Close the Case

I ship the verdict, keep a watchful eye on the numbers to be certain it holds, and file a thorough report in the casebook — so the next time a similar mystery turns up, the team already knows how it ends.
